Proteins
Proteins build and repair your muscles and tissues while regulating your body's daily processes! One gram of protein provides four calories. In a healthy, balanced diet, protein should contribute to 10-15% of daily calories.

Plant sources of protein include soybeans, quinoa, beans, lentils, soy products (e.g. tofu), and nuts

Animal food sources of protein include meat, poultry, eggs, seafood, and dairy products.

Fats
Fat insulates your body and organs, plays a role in your hormones, and provides needed acids in your body which it can't make itself! One gram of fat provides 9 calories - it is recommended that fat should contribute 20-30% of daily calories in a healthy diet.

Consuming large amounts of saturated fat can harm your body as well as increase your risk of Heart Attack or Stroke! Sources of saturated fat include meat, lard, and dairy products. Some examples of these include cheese, butter, sour cream, and milk.

Unsaturated fat, ie: "the good fat", has been linked to a reduced risk of cardiovascular desease. Unsaturated fat typically comes from plant oils, avacados, and nuts.
Of the two types: saturated and unsaturated, the majority of what you consume should be unsaturated.

Carbohydrates
Carboydrates are your body's main source of energy! Each gram of carbohydrates provides four calories. It is recommended that 50-65% of your daily intake comes from these sources.

Simple carbohydrates - also called sugars - usually have names ending in "-use" and tend to taste sweet. There are currently no formal guidelines for sugars in the diet, but nutrition experts agree it is best to restrict "added" sugars. Products with added sugars include processed foods, candy, and soft drinks.
Complex carbohydrates - also called starches - can stabilize your blood sugar. Sources include grain products (bread, cereal, rice, and pasta) as well as vegetables (potatoes and carrots) and legumes.
